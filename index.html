Introduction

We imagine the Catskill Interpretive Center taking on new roles in our interpretive design program. Not only will it will evolve into a place that provides an essential service to the area, but it will also emerge as a space that is intriguing, dynamic and sincere.

The Center will act as a mountain house, a ranger station as well as an observatory and research lab. Utilizing these roles in varying contexts, we are able to invent compelling interactions, tell fascinating stories and demonstrate the beauty of the Catskill region.

A few main inspirations helped guide our initial approaches for the Interpretive Center – the celebration, observation and engagement with nature and the rich history of Catskill tourism, mountain hospitality and ranger stations. These inspirations provided us with a level of color, charm and history found in the Catskills while also suggesting numerous visual cues and influence.

It’s important to note that these ideas should be treated as starting points for us to embark on larger conversations, interviews and research into crafting exactly the right quality of experiences at the Interpretive Center. While not every approach in this document will make its way into the overall interpretive program, we’re excited to present ways that the Center can begin to take shape.

Elevated Viewfinder

We became increasingly interested in experiences that directly connect visitors to the core idea of observation. Drawing on the experience and usages of the ‘lookout’ throughout the area, we want to create a large-scale elevated viewfinder, or periscope, that immediately transports visitors with an elevated view directly above the Center.
